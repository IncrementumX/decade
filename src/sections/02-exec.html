The assignment
Decade asks for an investment conviction on the role of Brazil’s tax-exempt assets — FIIs, Infra Debêntures, FI-Infra, FIAGRO, CRIs and CRAs (bank-issued LCI/LCA excluded) — in a client’s portfolio. The brief is explicit that these convictions both anchor client advice and teach the firm’s AI how it thinks about asset allocation and risk. Five questions must be answered, each supported by evidence with assumptions stated:
  1. Which types of tax-exempt assets should exist in the portfolio.
  2. Whether exposure should be implemented through direct instruments or funds (this may differ by asset type).
  3. How much should be allocated to each relevant bucket as a percentage of total wealth.
  4. Which specific assets are best-in-class to implement this exposure (actual assets).
  5. How client-specific factors (e.g. income stability, liquidity needs, existing real assets, risk tolerance) would impact your recommendations.
My Approach

Building a portfolio and underwriting across six different tax-exempt asset classes is not an easy task — especially in a country like Brazil. It requires a deep reading of the environment, depth where it actually matters, and relentless diligence. The content could easily become infinite.

Two mental models guided my work:

  • Reductionism: avoiding complexity loops — they generate entropy, energy that never converts into real work.
  • Reverse-thinking: starting each day thinking ex-ante about what went wrong — where to focus energy, which workflows broke — and ensuring it cannot happen in my process.

Statement 1 · The first mandate is survival
01

For any individual or family balance sheet, the first allocation mandate is survival. Whether the objective is absolute return, long-term purchasing power, or intergenerational wealth preservation, the portfolio must first avoid permanent impairment. This means diversifying risk, sizing adequation, and protecting the compounding base before trying to optimize returns.

"Rule No. 1: never lose money. Rule No. 2: never forget Rule No. 1."— Buffett's two rules
Statement 2 · Brazil makes fixed income the center of gravity
02

Pedro Malan often says that, in Brazil, even the past is uncertain — and he is right. We live with a deep inflationary memory — shaped by multiple stabilization plans and monetary regimes over time, structurally high real rates, recurring electoral binary risks, and shallow equity markets.

As a result, fixed income is the center of gravity of Brazilian allocation: the natural core of household portfolios, and almost the mirror image of the U.S., where households are primarily owners rather than creditors.

Statement 3 · Diversification and real returns are key
03

Tax-exempt assets — spanning exempt fixed-income credit (infra debêntures 12.431, FI-Infra, CRI, CRA) and exempt real-estate-equity / real-asset wrappers (FIIs and FIAGRO, which are not fixed income) — are powerful portfolio-construction resources — IF the pitfalls are avoided and they are assessed responsibly. They must do three jobs: (1) diversification (issuer, sector, index); (2) high net risk-adjusted returns (the IR exemption is the single largest controllable lever on net yield at a 14.25% Selic); and (3) inflation protection (IPCA-linked real carry, locked at a structurally rich entry — NTN-B real yield 8.18%).
