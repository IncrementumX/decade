Risk and return · the cost of compounding
The hierarchy only becomes useful paired with the path investors had to endure. CDI delivered the cleanest profile: high carry, positive real returns, minimal mark-to-market volatility. IMA-B and IMA-B5+ offered higher real returns, but that excess came from accepting duration risk and drawdowns. “Fixed income” in Brazil is not one low-risk bucket — CDI is the anchor, IMA-B5 is short-duration, IMA-B / IMA-B5+ are duration assets, IBOV TR is equity beta.

So what
The relevant test for a tax-exempt asset is not “does it yield more?” It is “does it compensate enough for the extra risk versus CDI and NTN-Bs?”
