From the hurdle to the assets
The macro conclusion is set: Brazil offers unusually high local carry and demands unusually high selectivity. CDI and NTN-Bs are not low hurdles — they are the baseline every incremental allocation must beat. This is where tax-exempt assets become relevant. For a taxable Brazilian individual, exemption can materially improve after-tax real returns — but only paired with the right asset, structure and manager. Each class in scope is assessed on the same question: does it improve the portfolio versus CDI and NTN-Bs after credit, duration, liquidity, governance, tax, structure and fee risk?
