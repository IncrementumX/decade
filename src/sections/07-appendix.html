Appendix · methodology & data layer
What lives where. Every figure in this case traces to an audited data and analytics layer; this tab describes its provenance, transforms and caveats.
  • Brazil-landscape series — rebased wealth (nominal and real), the real-rate regime, who-beats-CDI/IPCA hit-rates, drawdown and recovery, the FX/USD lens and the tax-equivalent bridge. Built from BCB-SGS (CDI, IPCA, PTAX), the ANBIMA IMA-B index family, B3 Ibovespa total return and Tesouro/BCB NTN-B references.
  • FII analytics — market series (estoque, investors, ADTV, issuance), segment composition, IFIX versus CDI / IMA-B / IMA-B5(+) performance (CAGR nominal and real, volatility, max drawdown, Sharpe), returns by real-rate regime, dividend yield by segment and the per-fund universe. Built from B3, ANBIMA and manager cartas; aggregator readings are marked as supplements.
  • Tax bridge — gross-up per instrument (FII income exempt, gain taxed at 20%; the regressive 22.5%→15% schedule on taxable carry), from the Receita Federal IR table and the underlying tax laws.
Methodology notes
  • Total return basis. All wealth indices include income (distributions / coupons / dividends) reinvested, so FIIs, bonds and equities compare on the same basis. IBOV = total return.
  • Real vs nominal. Real series are IPCA-deflated; drawdown is anchored on the nominal figure (what the screen showed), with real shown for cross-asset comparison.
  • Rolling windows. Overlapping monthly windows; observation counts differ by horizon & series start; descriptive frequency, not independent probability.
  • Proxies & gaps. Per-subtype FII total-return history and some logistics fund-level DY are proxied (IFIX sub-indices + DY) and flagged in-line. Residual gaps are noted where they appear in the body.
